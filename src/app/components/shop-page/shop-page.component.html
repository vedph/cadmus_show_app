Shopping for models

Welcome to the shop. This page is just a demonstrative list, to show the potential of an approach focusing on sharing and reusing composable data models and their editing user interfaces. As you can learn from the Cadmus data architecture, this project focuses on an open-ended set of independent, self-contained data models, the parts, which get composed into more complex items. This is a recipe for reuse, as not only data, but also the user interfaces for editing them can be shared and reused at will.

As a sample, here you will find most of the models defined and implemented for handling a number of different Cadmus-based projects. Hopefully, these models should give you an idea about their variety and their potential for reuse.

Shop's catalogs

Models are grouped into two catalogs: one for parts, another for fragments. From the point of view of the system, this distinction is artificial; as we always deal with just parts. Some of these parts are layer-parts: they contain a list of data (fragments), each linked to a specific portion of a text (in turn, contained in another part). Thus, layer parts all have the same model, as far as they are regarded as a list of fragments; yet, the model of each fragment is different, and that's what makes layer parts different.

Thus, we may have a comments layer part, where each fragment represents a generic comment attached to text; a paleographic layer part, where each fragment encodes the graphical features of letters, ligatures, etc.; and so forth.

The model of a layer part being a consequence of the model of its fragment, the list includes both part and fragment models. Yet, as explained here, fragment models really mean layer part models, as far as a layer part is just a set of fragments.

Each model has its own unique ID, listed with its user-friendly name, the name of the project it was developed for, and a set of tags. You can variously filter this list to find what you are looking for, or just browse it across its pages.

When you want to get some information about a model, just click its view button: at the bottom of the page you will find a short description, a strict formalization of it in a plain text, and one or more screenshots from its editing UI.
