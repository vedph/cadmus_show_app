The Models Shop

Cadmus is focused on reuse. This means not only software reuse; but also data models reuse.

In fact, there is no closed notion of a model for Cadmus records. Such records are defined by composing several models together, just like you can build any construction starting from a set of Lego-like bricks.

Often, real-world models are something complex, consisting of several parts, which may well appear in other models, too. For instance, consider the datation. This is not just a year, or a century number; it can be something very complex, like e.g. 367/366 B.C.? - ca. 150 A.D. (Roman copy), as it is found in SEG 46,1531. We thus need a model to adequately encode such datations in a structured, computable way.

The Shop

The shop here is just a demonstrative list. You will find all the models defined and implemented for handling a number of different Cadmus-based projects.

Models are grouped into two lists: one for parts, another for fragments. From the point of view of the system, this distinction is artificial; as we always deal with just parts. Some of these parts are layer-parts: they contain a list of data (fragments), each linked to a specific portion of a text (in turn, contained in another part). Thus, layer parts all have the same model, as far as they are regarded as a list of fragments; yet, the model of each fragment is different, and that's what makes layer parts different.

Thus, we may have a comments layer part, where each fragment represents a generic comment attached to text; a paleographic layer part, where each fragment encodes the graphical features of letters, ligatures, etc.; and so forth.

The model of a layer part being a consequence of the model of its fragment, the list includes both part and fragment models. Yet, as explained here, fragment models really mean layer part models, as far as a layer part is just a set of fragments.

Each model has its own unique ID, listed with its user-friendly name, the name of the project it was developed for, and a set of tags. You can variously filter this list to find what you are looking for, or just browse it across its pages.

When you want to get some information about a model, just click its view button: at the bottom of the page you will find a short description, a strict formalization of it in a plain text, and one or more screenshots from its editing UI.
